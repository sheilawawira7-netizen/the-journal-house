I’ve always believed that paper is one of the safest places on earth. It doesn't interrupt, it doesn't judge, and it never asks you to be anything other than exactly who you are in the moment.

With a background rooted in both the technical focus of computer engineering and the deep human connection of customer service, my favorite spaces have always been the ones where structure meets art. The Journal House was born out of a desire to create physical sanctuaries for the human experience.

When I sat down to design 'The Unwritten Things' and 'BECOMING ME,' I wasn't just thinking about paper weights or metallic foil accents. I was thinking about the person who would open them at 2:00 AM needing a safe place to breathe, reflect, and just heal.
